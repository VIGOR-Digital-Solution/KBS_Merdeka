§ 03 · Stakeholder architecture

Four seats at the table. Every seat wins visibly.

This is not a one-way funding request. Each of four audiences — KBS, YB ADUN, youth vendors, and proposed corporate partners — receives a defined, measurable outcome. The carnival is designed to only work if all four come to the table knowing exactly what they're getting.

01
KBS · Kementerian Belia & Sukan
Policy delivery, not spend.
A MADANI Belia flagship grounded in the Minister's own parliamentary seat (P.015 Sungai Petani) — measurable, replicable, reportable.
  • 300 youth income streams · direct attribution to KBS
  • 6 MADANI pillars each mapped to a delivered zone
  • Sports Scouting Pipeline · 50+ youth into state/national trials
  • Post-event KPI dashboard: jobs · GMV · skills · footfall · trials
  • Replicable template for future MADANI Belia carnivals
KBS Ask: RM2.5M allocation (including RM230K vendor subsidy) + programme endorsement + MSN/BAM/FAM scout coordination.

§ 05 · Zone architecture

Eight zones, each one a MADANI pillar made tangible.

300 youth vendors drawn from across the northern region — Kedah, Perlis, Penang, northern Perak — recruited through TEKUN, MARA, KICCI, KCCCI and JCI northern branches. Booth mix is calibrated to local supply: F&B-heavy, with a strong Handcraft heritage core and a focused 25-booth Digital & Creator zone for creator-economy entrepreneurs. Sports zones double as talent scouting stations feeding MSN and state team pipelines.

01·A
F&B · Halal Section
110 JAKIM-certified booths spanning heritage Laksa Kedah row, nasi kandar station, Malay-Mamak kopitiam pop-ups, and halal dessert startups. Activities: Santan pop-up kitchen with live nasi lemak demo, ZUS Barista School with RM10 trial certificate, Chef Wan Laksa Kedah judging Day 2, Tealive youth barista showdown, Usahawan Belia Awards (5 category prize pool), and a Mentorship Match for selected youth F&B founders.
